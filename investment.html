Investment Calculator — Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate my investment returns?

Use our free Investment Calculator above. Enter your initial investment, expected return rate, time period, and any regular contributions. The calculator shows your future value, total contributions, and net profit with or without inflation adjustment.

What is compound interest?

Compound interest means you earn interest on both your original investment AND on previously earned interest. This creates a snowball effect where your money grows faster over time. For example, at 8% annual return, your money roughly doubles every 9 years.

How does inflation affect my investment returns?

Inflation reduces the purchasing power of your money over time. A 10% nominal return with 3% inflation gives only about 7% real return. Our calculator adjusts for country-specific inflation rates so you see your true purchasing power gains.

What is the difference between simple and compound interest?

Simple interest is calculated only on the principal amount. Compound interest is calculated on the principal plus accumulated interest. Over long periods, compound interest produces significantly higher returns.

Should I invest with regular contributions?

Regular contributions (dollar-cost averaging) reduce the impact of market volatility and consistently grow your portfolio. Even small monthly contributions can build substantial wealth over time due to compound interest.
