Frequently Asked Questions - Commission Calculator

What are common commission structures in sales?

Common structures include straight commission (pay based entirely on sales), base plus commission (salary plus percentage of sales), tiered commission (rates increase with sales volume), and draw against commission (advance that is repaid from future commissions).

How do tiered commission structures work?

In a tiered structure, different commission rates apply to different levels of sales. For example, 3% on the first $50,000, 5% on $50,001-$100,000, and 7% above $100,000. This incentivizes higher performance while managing costs.

Is commission income taxed differently from salary?

Commission is typically taxed as ordinary income at the same rate as your salary. However, if you are an independent contractor receiving commissions, you may need to pay self-employment tax and make quarterly estimated tax payments.

How can I negotiate a better commission plan?

Research industry benchmarks for your role. Present data on your past performance and the value you bring. Propose specific terms such as a higher rate, lower threshold for bonuses, or uncapped commissions. Be prepared to discuss the mutual benefit.

What is a draw against commission?

A draw is a guaranteed advance on future commissions, typically paid during training or slow periods. It may be recoverable (you repay from future commissions) or non-recoverable (you keep it regardless). It provides income stability in commission-only roles.
