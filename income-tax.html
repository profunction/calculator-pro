Frequently Asked Questions - Income Tax Calculator

What is the difference between marginal and effective tax rate?

Your marginal tax rate is the rate applied to your last dollar of income (the highest bracket you reach). Your effective tax rate is the total tax divided by total income, which is always lower due to the progressive bracket system.

Should I take the standard deduction or itemize?

Take whichever is larger. For 2024, the standard deduction is $14,600 for single filers and $29,200 for married filing jointly. Itemize if your mortgage interest, state taxes, charitable donations, and medical expenses exceed the standard deduction.

How can I reduce my taxable income legally?

Max out tax-advantaged accounts (401k, IRA, HSA), harvest investment losses, donate appreciated assets to charity, use a flexible spending account, and consider tax-efficient investments like municipal bonds. Always consult a tax professional.

What tax bracket am I in?

Your tax bracket depends on your taxable income and filing status. For 2024, single filers earning $47,150-$100,525 fall in the 22% bracket. However, only the income within that bracket range is taxed at 22%, not all your income.

When are quarterly estimated tax payments required?

If you expect to owe $1,000 or more in taxes beyond what is withheld, you must make quarterly estimated payments. This commonly applies to self-employed individuals, freelancers, and those with significant investment or rental income.
