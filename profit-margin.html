Profit Margin Calculator — Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate profit margin?

Use our free Profit Margin Calculator above. Enter your revenue and costs. The calculator shows gross margin, operating margin, net profit margin, and markup percentage, with country-specific corporate tax deductions.

What is the difference between gross margin and net margin?

Gross margin = (Revenue - Cost of Goods Sold) / Revenue. Net margin = (Revenue - ALL expenses including taxes) / Revenue. Gross margin measures production efficiency, while net margin measures overall profitability after every cost.

What is markup vs margin?

Markup is the percentage added to cost to get the selling price. Margin is the percentage of the selling price that is profit. For example, a 50% markup on a $100 cost gives a $150 price, but the margin is only 33.3% ($50/$150). They are NOT the same.

How does corporate tax affect my profit?

Corporate tax reduces your net profit. Malaysia's corporate tax rate is 24% (15-17% for SMEs), US federal rate is 21%, China is 25% (15% for qualified small businesses). Our calculator shows profit after tax for your selected country.

What is a good profit margin?

A 'good' margin varies by industry. Retail: 2-5% net margin. Software/tech: 15-25%. Restaurants: 3-5%. Manufacturing: 5-10%. Generally, 10%+ net margin is considered healthy for most businesses.
